Tour Overview

When is it and what is the duration:
  • The ride starts on 4th June 2027 so we will be staying in Llanelli on the night before to allow an early start to the ride the next morning.
  • This tour is over a 3-day period finishing in Conwy on 7th June 2027, riding roads through the heart of Wales with 3 overnight stays.
  • The tour is primarily a road tour but occasionally uses well paved cycle paths that are suitable for road bikes.
  • The tour is limited to a maximum of 10 riders.
How much will it cost:
  • For three nights accommodation and bike/luggage transport, the estimated cost is around £300 per person.†
  • This estimate is based on a maximum group size of 10 riders and assumes shared twin-room accommodation.
  • Single-room accommodation can be arranged, depending on availability, for an additional charge.

    • †All our tours are designed to make no profit and any surplus funds are donated to our designated charity Rainbows Hospice.
What is included:
  • 3 nights' accommodation with breakfast.
  • Transportation to/from the start/end point for both bikes and luggage from a loading point in Belper - making it easier for you to arrange your own travel.
  • Transportation of luggage between accommodation stops - ride with just your essentials and not with the burden of excess weight.
  • A support vehicle to assist with any issues that may arise during the tour.†

    • † Each day, the support vehicle will meet riders at the ride half-way point.
What is not included:
  • Personal travel to/from the tour start/end point.†
  • Lunch / Dinner.
  • Insurance.

    • † We’ll offer guidance on travel options and group travel arrangements.

Stages / Routes ?

Where do we ride:
The routes are designed to take you through the most scenic parts of Wales. The first day is a gentle ride through the Elan Valley, the second day takes you over the Cambrian Mountains and to the West Coast and the third day takes you through Snowdonia.
All routes include options to switch to a shorter alternative with less elevation.

Day 1: Llanelli to Rhayader

The route starts in Llanelli and for 12 miles uses a disused railway line before navigating quiet country lanes passing Carreg Cennon Castle and reaching the historic village of Llandovery. It then uses quiet lanes over wild terrain through the Elan Valley to the pretty town of Rhayader for our overnight stay.

View Route

Day 2: Rhayader to Porthmadog

The route starts by making use of quiet roads across the Cambrian Mountains to the town of Machynlleth. Soon after we have a steep ascent through Corris before descending to Dolgellau. From there we take the Mawddach trail and cross the estuary via the impressive Barmouth Viaduct into the seaside town of Barmouth. We then continue along the coast through Harlech to Porthmadog for our overnight stay.

View Route

Day 3: Porthmadog to Conwy

The route starts with an optional detour up the switch back road to Stwlan Dam before passing through Ffestiniog and up to the Ffynnon Eidda viewpoint before the spectacular descent towards PenMachno and Betws-y-Coed. We then follow the Conwy valley before crossing Sychnant Pass to take the coast route to Conwy, our end destination. If time allows, riders can route to Llandudno and traverse the Great Orme before returning back to Conwy.

Transport & Rider Support ?

  • A support vehicle is available to transport your luggage between each overnight stop.
  • A support vehicle will be on-call if mechanical assistance or rider pick-up is needed.
  • With your luggage taken care of, you’re free to ride light and enjoy the day.

What to Bring ?

  • A well-serviced bike in good working order.
  • Helmet (mandatory).
  • Weather-appropriate cycling clothing.
  • Daily ride essentials (water, nutrition, tools).
  • Overnight bag – transported between each overnight stop by the support vehicle.
  • Any personal medication you may need.
