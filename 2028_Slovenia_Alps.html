Tour Overview

When is it?

Stages / Routes ?

Where do we ride?

The routes are designed to take you through the most scenic parts of the Slovenian Alpsand cross the border into Italy and Austria.
Routes may include options to switch to a shorter alternative with less elevation, or extensions if more miles are desired.
Each day, specific routes will be proposed, but may change to take account of weather conditions.

Day 1: Ljubljana circular

The route leaves Ljubljana and quickly transitions from the busy city to quiet countryside roads. You’ll ride mostly flat terrain with a few moderate climbs as we pass through the southern Polhov Gradec Dolomites. before returning to Ljubljana with time to explore the city.

View Route

Day 2: Ljubljana to Bohinj

From Ljubljana, we cycle through the historic town of Škofja Loka and continue into the Selca Valley before climbing over Soriška Planina and descending into Bohinj, where the day ends.

View Route

Day 3: Bohinj to Kranjska Gora

The day begins in the Julian Alps with a climb to the Pokljuka plateau, a vast spruce‑covered highland, before descending to Bled for a loop around the lake. From there, the peaceful Radovna Valley leads us to Mojstrana at the entrance to the Triglav valleys, and we finish by cycling through the scenic Upper Sava Valley to Kranjska Gora.

View Route

Day 4: Kranjska Gora Circular

Alpe Adria is one of Europe’s most scenic long‑distance cycling routes, and today we ride one of its finest Alpine stages. From Kranjska Gora, we cross into Italy and follow the old railway through the Kanal Valley, pass Dogna, then climb the Sella Nevea pass to Lake Predil. The long but steady route loops back to Kranjska Gora.

View Route

Day 5: Kranjska Gora to Tolmin

After a second night in Kranjska Gora, we head south over Slovenia’s highest road pass, the Vršič Pass. After its rewarding series of hairpins, we descend into the stunning Soča Valley and follow the river along a gentle, fast‑rolling road. With each kilometre, the climate turns more Mediterranean until we reach Tolmin, the largest town in the Upper Soča Valley.

View Route

Day 6: Tolmin to Ljubljana

On our final day, we loop back to Ljubljana to complete our own Tour of Slovenia. Crossing the Idrija region at the meeting point of the Alps and the Dinarides, we ride nearly 100 kilometres through its villages and ravines before the Ljubljana Basin opens ahead. A relaxed flat stretch across the Ljubljana Marshes brings us back to Slovenia’s capital.

What support is provided? ?

  • A support vehicle is available to transport your overnight luggage between each accommodation stop.
  • A support vehicle will be on-call if mechanical assistance or rider pick-up is needed.
  • With your overnight luggage taken care of, you’re free to ride light and enjoy the day.

What should I bring? ?

  • A well-serviced bike in good working order or organise a rental bike.
  • Helmet (mandatory).
  • Weather-appropriate cycling clothing.
  • Daily ride essentials (water, nutrition, tools).
  • Any personal medication you may need.
