Tour Overview

Reserve a place

When is it and what is the duration:
This tour is over a 3-day period riding roads across the Scottish Lowlands with 3 overnight stays. The ride starts on 17th September 2027 so we will be staying in Edinburgh on the night before to be able start the ride early the next morning.

The tour is primarily a road tour but does use well paved cycle paths that are suitable for road bikes.

What else do I need to know:
Bikes and luggage will be transported from Belper to the start point and back home to Belper from the end point. Luggage will be transported between each overnight stop.
This makes it easier for you to arrange your own travel to/from the start/end point and enables you to cycle without the burden of carrying luggage.

We’ll offer guidance on travel options and group travel arrangements.

How much will it cost:
For three nights accommodation and bike/luggage transport, the estimated cost is approximately £300 per person. This is based on a maximum group size of 10 riders and assumes shared twin rooms. Single room arrangements can be accommodated, with a supplement for single room use.

All our tours are designed to make no profit and any surplus funds are donated to our designated charity Rainbows Hospice.

Routes ?

Where do we ride:
The routes are designed to take you through the most scenic parts of the Scottish Lowlands. The first day crosses the Forth road bridge, skirting the Firth of Forth estuary before turning west to Stirling, the second day, to the Trossachs, Loch Lomond and Glasgow and the third takes you back to Edinburgh via the Clyde and Firth Canal.
All routes include options to switch to a shorter alternative with less elevation.

Day 1: Edinburgh to Stirling

The route starts in Edinburgh and crosses the Forth road bridge and skirts the Firth of Forth Estuary before heading inland through Dunfermline and onto the historic town of Stirling for our overnight stay.

View Route

Day 2: Stirling to Glasgow

From Stirling, the route makes its way towards the Trossachs before turning south towards Balloch and the banks of Loch Lomond. It then follows scenic paths by the River Leven into Glasgow for our overnight stay.

View Route

Day 3: Glasgow to Edinburgh

We route through Glasgow using cycle paths before joining the cycle path on the Forth and Clyde Canal with stops at the Falkirk Wheel and the Kelpies. The route then makes its way through Falkirk and onto Edinburgh passing Blackness Castle on the way.

Accommodation ?

Where do we stay:
Bed and Breakfast accommodation is provided for all riders at each overnight stop. We encourage riders to share a twin room to keep costs reasonable, but riders will have the option for a single room subject to a supplement.
Accommodation preferences will be collected and booked by the ride organisers.

Venues for evening meals will be provided nearer the time.

Detailed Information

Everything organised so you can focus on the ride.
Here’s what you need to know about travel, support and what to bring.

Travel Information ?

  • Riders will organise and pay for their own travel arrangements to and from the start/finish destinations.
  • Bikes and luggage will be transported by our support vehicle to and from the start/finish destinations, allowing easier travel arrangements for riders.
  • We will communicate travel arrangements to help coordinate riders who want to travel together.
  • The start and finish locations are identical, so for this tour, travel by car is possible if organised with long stay parking. Our recommended travel option for both the outbound and return journweys is by rail, with direct connections between Derby and Edinburgh:

Transport & Rider Support ?

  • A support vehicle is available to transport your luggage between each overnight stop.
  • A support vehicle will be on-call if mechanical assistance or rider pick-up is needed.
  • With your luggage taken care of, you’re free to ride light and enjoy the day.

What to Bring ?

  • A well-serviced bike in good working order.
  • Helmet (mandatory).
  • Weather-appropriate cycling clothing for changeable conditions.
  • Daily ride essentials (water bottles, nutrition, basic tools, spares).
  • Overnight bag – this will be transported to and from the start/finish and between each overnight stop by the support vehicle.
  • Any personal medication you may need.
