2027 June â€“ Wales, South to North

Overview

A very scenic tour starting in Llanelli on the coast in South Wales, skirting the Brecon Beacons and the Elan Valley, through the heart of Wales and Snowdonia to finish in Conwy on the North Wales coast. Using country lanes and well paved cycle paths, some parts of this route are challenging, but with stunning views and rewarding stops along the way.

Tour Dates ?

4th - 8th June 2027

Tour Metrics

Distance Elevation

Route Info ?

Day 1 - Llanelli to Rhayader:

The ride starts in Llanelli and uses a disused railway line, passing Carreg Cennon Castle before reaching Llandovery. It then passes over the Elan Valley to the pretty town of Rhayader for our overnight stay.

View Route Details

Day 2 - Rhayader to Porthmadog:

The ride starts using quiet roads to the town of Machynlleth. After this there is a steep ascent through Corris before descending to Dolgellau, where we take the Morfa Mawddach trail and cross the estuary via the impressive railway bridge into Barmouth. We then continue along the coast through Harlech to Porthmadog for our overnight stay.

View Route Details

Day 3 - Porthmadog to Conwy:

The ride starts by taking us through Ffestiniog and up to the Ffynnon Eidda viewpoint before descending towards PenMachno and Betws-y-Coed and then following the Conwy valley down to the coast at Conwy, our end destination.

2027 August â€“ Scottish Lowlands

Overview

A circular route starting and ending near Edinburgh, passing through Stirling and Glasgow.

  • Day 1 - Champany to Stirling: The ride started by visiting the 3 bridge viewpoint in Queensferry before crossing the Forth road bridge and riding the shoreline of the Firth Estuary making use of cycle paths through Dunfermline to Stirling.
  • Day 2 - Stirling to Glasgow: The route headed to Balloch on the shores of Loch Lomond, where some riders chose the detour to cross by ferry from Balmaha to Luss, before continuing along cycle paths beside the River Leven to Dumbarton, then turning east into Glasgow to follow the River Clyde.
  • Day 3 - Glasgow to Champany: The return to Champany traced quiet cycle paths and a sweeping stretch beside the Forth and Clyde Canal, with opportunities to pause at the Falkirk Wheel, admire the Kelpies, and explore the historic Blackness Castle along the way.

2017 â€“ Coast to Coast

Overview

One of the classic Sustrans Coast to Coast routes from the Irish Sea to the North Sea, crossing the Lake District, Pennines, and Durham Dales.

  • Day 1 - Whitehaven to Penrith: The route starts on the West coast and took us through the Northern part of the lake district, over Whinlatter Pass and into Keswick. 2014 â€“ Coast and Castles

Overview

The Coast and Castles cycle route is a Sustrans long-distance ride from Newcastle to Edinburgh, following the Northumberland and Scottish coastlines past historic castles, fishing villages, and dramatic scenery.

  • Day 1 - Newcastle to Seahouses: The group left Newcastle leaving Kev behind to meet Martyn at the train station who both starting cycling 90mins behind the main group. Everyone taking the same route out to the coast at Tynemouth continuing with the ride hugging the coast through Whitley Bay, Blyth, Amble. Eventually everyone regrouped for lunch at Alnmouth before continuing to finish the ride at Seahouses.
  • Day 2 - Seahouses to Dunbar: After leaving Seahouse and and passing Bamburgh Castle, 3 riders pressed on to visit Lindisfarne on Holy Island (Laurie, Mark & Martyn) - the main group choosing to continue along the coastal path. All riders regrouped for lunch in Berwick upon Tweed, before continuing on to the Day 2 stopover in Dunbar.
  • Day 3 - Dunbar to Edinburgh: The day started with breakfast in the conservatory with a great view of the seals basking on the rocks in the early morning sun. The riders left Dunbar and followed the coast to Musselburgh for a relaxed lunch before continuing at a steady pace onto our final destination of Edinburgh.
